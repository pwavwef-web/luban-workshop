# Luban Workshop Restaurant Campus Campaign

## Campaign title
**Taste Luban: Your Campus Chinese Lunch Spot**

## Campaign goal
Increase awareness, walk-ins, reservations, and pickup orders among UCC students, lecturers, staff, and the wider Cape Coast campus community.

The existing site already positions Luban Workshop Restaurant as an authentic Chinese restaurant in Cape Coast, located at Cafe Roof Top, Casford Street, UCC, with online reservations and menu access. The campaign should therefore push one very clear behaviour: **see the food, scan the QR code, visit the restaurant, and order/reserve.**

## Core audience
1. **Students** looking for affordable, interesting lunch options between classes.
2. **Lecturers and staff** looking for a calm lunch space or quick pickup.
3. **Small groups** looking for casual hangouts, birthday meals, student association lunch-outs, or department meetups.
4. **Curious first-timers** who have heard of Luban but do not know what to order.

## Big idea
Most campus restaurants sell food. Luban sells a small escape: warm noodles, rice, soups, seafood, beef dishes, Chinese flavour, rooftop energy, and a “let me try something new today” feeling.

**Campaign promise:**
> A proper Chinese lunch experience is already on campus.

## Main message pillars
- **Convenience:** Located at UCC, Cafe Roof Top, Casford Street.
- **Curiosity:** Try authentic Chinese meals without leaving campus.
- **Social proof:** Bring your friends, classmates, course mates, or department squad.
- **Ease:** Scan the QR code, view the menu, reserve, or plan pickup.
- **Visual hunger:** Let food photos carry the campaign. The food must do the shouting.

- Name: **Luban Workshop Restaurant**

## Campaign look and feel
**Style:** warm, bold, appetising, premium but still student-friendly.

**Recommended colours:**
- Deep red / Chinese restaurant red
- Cream / warm white
- Charcoal / black
- Gold accent

**Typography direction:**
- Use a bold serif for headlines where possible.
- Use a clean sans-serif for details, prices, opening hours, and calls to action.

**Visual rule:**
One strong food image per design. Do not clutter. Campus flyers die when they look like someone fought Canva and lost.

# Photoshoot plan

## Photoshoot concept
**“Luban Lunch Moments”**

Capture the restaurant as a campus lunch destination: students ordering, friends sharing, close-up food shots, staff serving, and relaxed rooftop dining.

## Shot list

### 1. Hero food spread
- Overhead shot of noodles, rice, soup, beef/seafood dish, chopsticks, drinks, and small plates.
- Use `assets/luban-noodle-spread.jpg` as visual reference.
- Purpose: main flyer, website hero, Instagram post.

### 2. Student lunch table
- 3–5 students around one table sharing food.
- One person scanning the QR code or checking the menu on phone.
- Mood: warm, laughing, natural.
- Purpose: social media carousel, campus poster.

### 3. Lecturer/staff lunch moment
- One or two smartly dressed people having a calm lunch.
- Mood: quiet, comfortable, professional.
- Purpose: staff-targeted WhatsApp poster.

### 4. First bite reaction
- Close-up of a student tasting noodles or dumplings.
- Expression should be genuine, not theatrical.
- Purpose: reels, TikTok, meme-style content.

### 5. Kitchen/service action
- Staff plating food, carrying tray, serving dish, pouring soup.
- Purpose: credibility and behind-the-scenes content.

### 6. Exterior/location proof
- Signage or entrance shot showing where Luban is located.
- Include a student walking toward the restaurant.
- Purpose: reduce “where is it?” confusion.

### 7. QR code interaction
- Student holding flyer or phone, scanning the QR code.
- Purpose: CTA graphic and digital flyer.

### 8. Bao mascot integration
- Use Bao mascot assets on digital flyers and social posts.
- Do not overuse Bao on premium food shots. Bao should guide, not hijack the plate.

## Photoshoot props
- Printed QR code flyer
- Chopsticks
- Clean bowls and plates
- Red napkins or table accents
- Phone showing the website/menu
- UCC student bag or books for campus identity

## Model direction
- Students should dress normally but cleanly.
- Avoid overly formal posing.
- Use diverse groups: friends, couples, solo lunch, lecturer/staff.
- Capture movement: reaching for food, laughing, scanning QR, entering restaurant.

# Flyer production checklist

## Digital sizes
- Instagram post: 1080 x 1080
- Instagram story / WhatsApp status: 1080 x 1920
- Facebook post: 1200 x 630
- Printable A4 flyer: 2480 x 3508 px at 300 DPI

## Must-have elements on every flyer
- Luban Workshop Restaurant logo
- One strong food image
- Clear headline
- Location
- Opening days and time
- Phone number
- QR code
- Website

## Avoid
- Too much text
- Too many menu items on one flyer
- Low-contrast text over food images
- Using every asset at once
- Making the mascot bigger than the food

# Success metrics
Track these weekly:
- Average daily customers
- QR code scans / website visits
- Menu page visits
- Reservation clicks/submissions
- Calls from flyer campaign
- Instagram profile visits
- WhatsApp shares

Move from about 30 average daily customers to **45–60 daily customers** within 4 weeks through consistent flyer distribution, class-group sharing, and food-led content.
