# Canva brand setup

## Canva canvas sizes
Create these five designs:

1. **Instagram / Facebook square**
   - Size: 1080 x 1080 px
   - Use for Instagram feed, Facebook, WhatsApp image posts.

2. **WhatsApp / Instagram story**
   - Size: 1080 x 1920 px
   - Use for WhatsApp status, Instagram stories, TikTok story posts.

3. **A4 printable flyer**
   - Size: A4 portrait
   - Export as PDF Print.

4. **Landscape social banner**
   - Size: 1200 x 630 px
   - Use for Facebook, link preview, and website promotional banners.

5. **Mini handbill**
   - Size: A5 portrait
   - For campus handouts.

## Colour palette
Use this palette for all campaign designs:

- Deep restaurant red: `#B91C1C`
- Dark wine red: `#7F1D1D`
- Charcoal: `#1C1917`
- Warm cream: `#FAFAF9`
- Soft red highlight: `#FECA CA` without the space: `#FECACA`
- Gold accent: `#D4AF37`

## Font direction in Canva
Use accessible Canva fonts:

### Headline fonts
Choose one:
- Playfair Display Bold
- Cormorant Garamond Bold
- Libre Baskerville Bold

### Body fonts
Choose one:
- Lato
- Open Sans
- Montserrat
- Source Sans 3

## General design rules
- Food image should cover 55–70% of the design.
- Use one major food image per flyer.
- Put the QR code in a clean cream or white box.
- Keep text big enough to read on WhatsApp.
- Do not put red text directly on red food. Design is not hide-and-seek.
- Mascot should support the design, not dominate it.

# Photoshoot mood board

## Mood board title
**Campus Chinese Lunch — Warm, Social, Appetising**

## Visual keywords
- Rooftop lunch
- Warm plates
- Chinese cuisine
- Student groups
- Friendly service
- Lunch break energy
- Food close-ups
- Calm staff lunch
- QR code interaction

## Lighting style
Use bright natural light. Avoid harsh flash. Food should look warm, not hospital-bright.

## Composition style
- Close-up food shots: 45-degree angle and overhead.
- People shots: medium shots, natural laughs, real table interaction.
- Location shots: wide enough to show space and direction.
- Service shots: hands serving, plates moving, steam if possible.

## Outfit direction
### Students
- Casual campus outfits
- Clean shirts, jeans, dresses, backpacks, notebooks
- Avoid loud brand logos

### Lecturers/staff
- Smart casual
- Button-down shirts, blouses, neat dresses, simple office wear

### Staff/servers
- Clean aprons or uniform if available
- Hair neat, hands clean, plating area tidy

## Props
- Chopsticks
- Napkins
- Red table accents
- QR code card
- Phone showing menu page
- Notebooks/laptop for campus identity
- Drinks
- Small plates

# Photoshoot shot-by-shot plan

## Shot 1: Hero food spread
**Angle:** overhead 90 degrees  
**Framing:** all dishes visible  
**Use for:** main flyer and website banner  
**Direction:** bowls and plates should feel abundant but neat.

## Shot 2: Noodle pull
**Angle:** close-up 45 degrees  
**Action:** chopsticks lifting noodles  
**Use for:** reels, story posts, student flyer  
**Direction:** shoot multiple takes; noodle shots are divas.

## Shot 3: Student group lunch
**Angle:** medium-wide  
**Action:** friends sharing food and laughing  
**Use for:** group flyer and Instagram carousel  
**Direction:** avoid fake laughter. Ask them to actually talk.

## Shot 4: Scan and order
**Angle:** over-the-shoulder  
**Action:** student scans QR code from flyer  
**Use for:** CTA posts  
**Direction:** phone screen should be bright enough to see.

## Shot 5: Staff serving dish
**Angle:** side close-up  
**Action:** server places dish on table  
**Use for:** trust-building content  
**Direction:** capture hands, plate, customer smile.

## Shot 6: Lecturer lunch
**Angle:** medium shot  
**Action:** lecturer/staff seated with meal  
**Use for:** staff flyer  
**Direction:** keep mood calm and mature.

## Shot 7: Location finder
**Angle:** wide shot  
**Action:** person walking toward restaurant  
**Use for:** “How to find us” post  
**Direction:** include recognizable surroundings.

## Shot 8: Bao plus food composite
**Angle:** digital design, not physical photo  
**Action:** place Bao beside food photo in Canva  
**Use for:** mascot flyer and social ads.

# Canva export checklist

Before exporting, confirm every design includes:

- Logo
- Food image
- Clear headline
- Location
- Opening days and hours
- QR code
- Phone number
- Website
- Enough contrast
- No tiny unreadable text
- No stretched food photo

## Export settings
### For WhatsApp / Instagram
- File type: PNG
- Size: 1x or 2x
- Transparent background: off

### For print
- File type: PDF Print
- Crop marks and bleed: on if sending to a professional printer
- Colour: RGB is acceptable for campus printing, but CMYK is preferred if printer supports it

# Final recommendation
Start with three designs only:

1. Main awareness flyer
2. Student lunch flyer
3. WhatsApp story version

Do not create ten designs at once. Launch three strong assets, test reactions for one week, then refine. Marketing without testing is just decoration with confidence.
